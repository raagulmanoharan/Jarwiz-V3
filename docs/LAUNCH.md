# Launch kit — Product Hunt

A ready-to-run playbook for launching Jarwiz on Product Hunt, distilled from
current (2025–2026) launch research. The through-line: **lead with the artifact
and the transformation, never with the word "AI."** On a PH flooded with AI
wrappers, "AI-powered" in your headline reads as *wrapper*; a board building
itself reads as *magic*.

## The positioning, in one breath

Anchor to a category people already fund (the whiteboard / canvas), win on the
verb (**it does the thinking, you don't**). Keep "the thinking board" as the
category name to grow into later — don't lead with an invented category while
the UX is still unfamiliar.

## Tagline (60-char limit)

Lead with a **provocative launch title** + a **plain what-it-does tagline**
(the split Chronicle used to hit #1: title *"Cursor for Slides"*, tagline
*"Stunning presentations with AI. No design skills required."*).

- **Launch title:** `A collaborator, not a chatbot in a sidebar` (43)
- **Tagline:** `Type a subject. Get a whole strategy board.` (43)

Backups to A/B:
- `AI that builds a board, not a chat transcript.` (47)
- `SWOTs, personas, matrices — built by AI on a canvas.` (51)
- `Hand it a goal. It researches and builds the board.` (52)

## First gallery image = launch poster = social share image

It's the OG image too, so it must stand alone. Product name + one line + a
framed screenshot of a **real generated board** (reuse
`site/assets/example-board.png` or a fresh one). ~1270×760, 10–20% padding,
readable as a thumbnail, answers "what is this?" in ~2 seconds.

## Gallery order

1. **Poster** (static) — name + one-liner + a real board.
2. **The transformation** (animated WebM) — the hero loop: blank canvas → type a
   goal → cards fan out into a board. This is the product; it's the most
   important asset. (The site's `?embed=1` view already plays exactly this — screen-record it.)
3. **A Thinking Machine running** (animated or static) — pick a subject, cards
   appear with citations.
4. **Payoff shots** (static) — a finished SWOT board; a single polished card;
   the Effort–Impact matrix.
5. **Closing slide** (static) — "Open source · runs in your browser" + CTA.

Use **WebM over GIF** (smaller at equal quality), keep animated slides < ~8 MB,
and don't make it *all* motion — static wins for the finished end-states.

## Thumbnail

240×240. A bold mark, not the full wordmark. High contrast; legible on both the
light and dark PH feed.

## Maker's first comment (300–400 words)

Structure: origin story (the itch — "I was tired of copy-pasting ChatGPT walls
of text into a doc I still had to organize") → who it's for (founders/PMs/
strategists) → 3 core benefits (board not chat · real researched frameworks ·
Autopilot builds it from a goal) → what's different (open source, in-browser,
agentic — the things a wrapper can't fake) → **one specific feedback ask**.
Never "please upvote" — the community penalizes it.

## Beating AI-fatigue

Your three credibility signals wrappers can't fake — lean on all of them:

- **Open source** (repo link, self-hostable, MIT).
- **In-browser** (no install).
- **Agentic** (it does multi-step work and researches the live web).

Mobilize the GitHub/dev community specifically — that audience rewards "not
another wrapper, and you can read the code."

## Mechanics

- **When:** 12:01 AM PST, **Tue–Thu** (avoid Mon/Fri/weekends).
- **Goal:** crack Top 4 in the first ~4 hours (early rank compounds; counts are
  hidden early).
- **Hunter:** self-hunting is fine — ~79% of featured posts are self-hunted.
- **Warm-up:** complete + age the maker profile ~30 days out; build a "notify me
  on launch" teaser 1–4 weeks ahead; prime your email list / X / Discord.
- **On the day:** whole team replies to every comment fast. Share the *link*;
  don't DM for upvotes (it can get you penalized).
- **Capture proof:** the first 48 hours are the most valuable social-proof window
  you'll ever get — screenshot every reply, comment, and mention. Filter later.

## After launch — feed the site

Pipe the best captured quotes back into the landing page (beta-labeled is fine
and adds authenticity), and add a GitHub-stars signal once the count is
flattering.
