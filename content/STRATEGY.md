# Content Strategy — "Build What You'd Actually Use"

An Instagram weekly series documenting the full build of Jarwiz — an AI canvas tool — from first principles. Equal parts product thinking, engineering, and honest founder reflection.

## The premise

Most "build with AI" content shows polished demos or vague tips. This series is different: one real product, built in public, one week at a time. You see the decisions, the dead ends, the moments where something clicks.

## Target audience

- Builders (developers, PMs, founders) who are tired of AI hype and want to see real product thinking
- People experimenting with AI tools who want to understand what's actually worth building
- Early-career engineers who want to see how product decisions get made in practice

## Format per episode

- **Length:** 60–90 seconds (Instagram Reels)
- **Structure:** Hook (0–5s) → Setup (5–20s) → Substance (20–70s) → CTA (70–90s)
- **Tone:** Direct, no hype, genuine builder voice. Show the thinking, not just the result.
- **Visuals:** Screen recordings of the actual canvas + talking head (HeyGen). No stock footage.
- **Cadence:** Weekly drop, same day/time

## Series name (options)

- "Build What You'd Actually Use"
- "In the Build" (with Jarwiz)
- "Canvas" (double meaning: blank canvas + the tool)
- "Ship It" — too generic

## What makes this different

1. It's one real product, not a grab-bag of tips
2. The product is genuinely interesting (AI canvas, multiplayer agents, streaming presence)
3. The builder is honest about what's hard and what failed
4. Each episode teaches something concrete — a decision, a pattern, a tradeoff
