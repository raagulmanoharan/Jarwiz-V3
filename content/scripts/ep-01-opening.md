**Format:** Instagram Reel, 75 seconds

## HeyGen Prompt

## Script

*[Talking head — casual, no desk, minimal background]*

Every AI tool I've tried puts the AI in a box.

You type in one end. You get text out the other. You copy it somewhere else. And you do that over and over until you've got something useful.

That's not how thinking actually works.

*[Cut to Jarwiz canvas — empty board, then drop a PDF]*

I'm building something called Jarwiz. It's an infinite canvas — like FigJam — except the AI agents live on the canvas with you. They don't reply in a chat. They place cards. You can see them working. The output lands next to your notes, connected to the source it came from, in a space you actually own.

*[Show the Summarizer avatar gliding, text streaming into a doc card]*

This is what it looks like when an agent reads a PDF and writes its analysis directly onto the board.

That cursor isn't decorative. That's the agent. And that text — streaming in live — is the artifact. Not a message. A card you can move, build on, or hand to a different agent.

*[Back to talking head]*

I'm building this in public. Every week I'm going to show you one layer of how it works — the decisions, the dead ends, the moments where something finally clicks.

Not tips. Not demos of someone else's API wrapper. One real product, built from scratch, one week at a time.

If you're the kind of person who builds things you actually want to use — this series is for you.

Follow. First drop next week.

---

## Director notes for HeyGen

- **Tone:** Calm, direct. Like a founder explaining to another founder — not pitching, not performing.
- **Pace:** Slightly slower than you think. The canvas cuts need time to breathe.
- **B-roll timing:** 
  - "I'm building something called Jarwiz" → cut to canvas
  - "This is what it looks like" → hold on streaming card for 4–5 seconds, don't rush
  - "Not tips. Not demos." → back to talking head
- **Avatar style:** Minimal gestures. Eye contact. Not stiff — just focused.
- **No music under the first half.** Subtle low ambient under the canvas demo section only.
